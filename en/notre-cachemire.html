The origin

Inner Mongolia,
cradle of the finest cachemire.

Cachemire comes from the down of Capra Hircus goats raised on the high plateaux of Inner Mongolia. At this altitude, extreme temperature swings — down to –40°C in winter — push these goats to develop an undercoat of exceptional fineness and softness.

Each goat produces only 150 to 200 grams of cachemire per year. It is this rarity, combined with the quality of the fibre, that explains the value of the material. For a single MAUDÉ coat, several goats contribute their annual down.

We work with partners established in this region for decades, guaranteeing complete traceability of the fibre.

The making

Suzhou,
2,500 years of textile.

Nicknamed the "Venice of the Orient", Suzhou has been the world capital of precious textile since antiquity. Imperial silk, fine embroidery, worked cachemire — everything requiring patience and precision finds its masters here.

Our partner ateliers in Suzhou master the art of cachemire weaving through techniques passed down from generation to generation. Each MAUDÉ coat goes through more than 40 production stages before it is ready to travel to you.

We regularly visit our ateliers. We know the people who make your coats. This is a relationship of trust, not an anonymous order.

From fibre to coat

A journey in three steps.

01

The harvest

In spring, herders gently comb the goats' undercoat by hand. The down is then sorted, cleaned and combed to separate the longest and finest fibres — only these will enter the composition of our coats.

02

Spinning & weaving

The fibres are spun, dyed in meticulously controlled baths, then woven with the selected laine to create our 50/50 blend. This process demands clockmaker's precision: thread tension, fabric density and final weight are checked at every stage.

03

The making

The fabric is then cut and sewn by our artisans in Suzhou. Each piece is individually inspected before dispatch. Seams, buttonholes, drape, finishings — nothing is left to chance. A MAUDÉ coat leaves the atelier only when it is perfect.

What you
actually want to know.

Why blend laine and cachemire rather than 100% cachemire?

100% cachemire is spectacular to the touch — but it is fragile. Laine provides resistance and structure that cachemire alone cannot guarantee in a coat worn daily. Our 50/50 blend combines the best of both: the softness of cachemire, the durability of laine.

How do you know if cachemire is quality?

A superior fibre measures less than 15 microns in thickness — for comparison, a human hair measures 70 microns. The finer the fibre, the softer and lighter the fabric. We select exclusively long and fine fibres, which limits pilling and extends the lifespan of the coat.

Will the coat pill?

All cachemire, even the finest, may pill slightly at first — this is a sign that residual short fibres are naturally shedding. After a few wears, the phenomenon disappears and the fabric reveals its definitive softness. Use a gentle cachemire stone to remove any pills if needed.

How long will my MAUDÉ coat last?

With proper care, a quality wool and cachemire coat can be worn 10 to 15 years. This is the opposite of fast fashion: you pay more once, but you divide the cost by ten. A MAUDÉ coat worn for 10 years works out at less than £40 per year.

Caring for your coat

The essential gestures so it lasts.

Air rather than wash

Between wears, hang your coat in fresh air overnight. Cachemire self-regenerates — the natural humidity of the air is enough to smooth the fibres.

Gentle washing

If washing is necessary: by hand in cold water with a wool detergent, or a gentle machine cycle in a mesh bag. Never wring. Dry flat.

Off-season storage

Fold your coat, do not hang it (the weight distorts the shoulders). Store it clean in a cotton cover with a few cedar leaves to repel moths.

Pilling

Use a cachemire stone or soft comb. Lay the coat flat, gently brush the surface. Pills disappear in a few passes.
